Our Team

Matt Campbell

Managing Director

Matt Campbell brings military and technician training to excel as Managing Director at Gemini Energy Services. Before joining Gemini, Matt enlisted in the Navy’s nuclear propulsion training program as an electrician on the USS Alaska, a ballistic missile submarine. He completed deterrent patrols, a change of homeport and a complete reactor refueling. In 2012, Matt joined Gemini as a field service technician specializing in photovoltaic inverters and transitioned into wind energy. With an extensive industrial background, Matt currently coordinates between customers and technicians to ensure quality wind energy projects are completed.

Alan Tennery

Director of Engineering, Wind Operations & Maintenance

Alan comes to Tetra Tech with nearly 20 years of engineering experience across commercial aerospace, environmental science and renewable energy industries, where he has worked in startups, non-profits and large corporations. A creative thinker and problem-solver, Alan has led systems engineering for a continental scale environmental observatory measuring climate data, managed test programs validating design and performance characteristics of wind turbines, and overseen flight control and landing gear maintenance and reliability modification projects for commercial aerospace. Alan obtained his B.Sc. degree in Engineering from California State University – Sacramento.

Chris Lundquist

Project Manager

Chris joins the Gemini team from one of Tetra Tech’s engineering design offices in Denver, Colorado. He has over 13 years of progressive experience with a variety of power generation projects including combined cycle/simple cycle natural gas, bio-fuel, wind, solar and supercritical coal power plants. He has an extensive background in utility scale engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) projects including the successful completion of several industry recognized turnkey EPC projects, some with a project value of over $1 billion. Chris obtained his B.Sc. degree in mechanical engineering from the University of Colorado and is a licensed professional engineer in 8 states.

Julius Pachy

EHS Manager

Julius Pachy came to Gemini Energy Services in 2023 with over 20 years of Environmental, Health and Safety (EHS) experience within the automotive manufacturing industry, oil and gas sector and recently in vehicle traffic transportation and military defense work. Julius career has centered around the creation and support of EHS programs, namely, reviewing and creating programs to meet the health and safety needs of the staff while ensuring compliance with the specific federal agencies. Julius’ number one goal is the safety of all employees he works with (field and office) as well and protecting individuals who work around or with Gemini employees. His talents will contribute to his desire to create a safety culture that emphasizes Goal Zero (nobody gets hurt), safety at work and at home as well as educating individuals, teams, companies to ensure everyone understands the value behind EHS.

Frank Goeseke

Specialty Service Manager

Michael English

Resource Manager

Michael English is a seasoned Project Manager at Gemini Energy Services.  He joined Gemini Energy Services in 2012. He initially joined the company as a field service technician after completing an impressive 22-year career in the U.S. Navy as an Electronic Technician. During his military service, Mike deployed multiple times in support of key operations, including Operation Enduring Freedom and Operation Iraqi Freedom, working with specialized teams such as the Naval Special Warfare Mobile Communications Team, Naval Special Warfare Special Boat Unit, USS Ronald Reagan, and USS New Orleans. With extensive technical experience in various sectors, Mike has worked on projects involving solar energy, wind farm operations and maintenance support, retrofits, design modifications, and commissioning. In his current position as Project Manager, he oversees a diverse range of projects with leading manufacturers, including Siemens, Gamesa, Nordex, Acciona, and Pattern Energy. Mike also manages a team of over 30 technicians, ensuring the successful execution of complex energy projects.
